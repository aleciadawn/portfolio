Miri Music Website


Miri Music is an up-and-coming recording studio in the Denver Metro Area that was looking to translate its welcoming, creative atmosphere into a digital online presence.
Role
I acquired this project after consulting on the original version of the company's public website. Because of this, I had a timeline of three weeks to design, build, and deploy the site. I started with some basic user interviews to see what potential clients would look for when choosing to book a studio and followed that up with competitive analysis to better understand how Miri stood out from the competition. Taking my findings and time constraint into consideration, I created a bold, informative site to allow for quick and easy booking.
Project Challenges
The short timeline meant fewer workflow iterations and less testing than I typically would have on any given project. I mitigated this issue by moving forward on the design and development process while simultaneously conducting user interviews and making adjustments as needed.
Client Requirements
As a new business in Denver, this client needed an inexpensive website to showcase their studio, staff, and services. Most importantly they were looking for a primary location to send clients for easy booking.
UX Goals
The goal was to offer a potential client a place to easily book a session and answer any questions they may have about the studio as efficiently as possible, regardless of their device.
Home Page Layout
The home page layout is designed so that the user can acheive any goal in under 60 seconds. The primary user goals were to book the studio, see photos and specs, learn about services and pricing, and review work examples.
Open Source Framework
The site is built using the Bootstrap framework. Bootstrap's responsive grid system, easily editable components, and flexible libraries meant I could build the site quickly with confidence that another developer could come in after me and pick up easily.
HTML | CSS | jQuery
The original site was build using Wordpress. I made the decision to remove the site from Wordpress and build a static site using HTML, CSS, and jQuery. This allowed for more flexibility in the site desgin and a lighter website.
Security
Miri needed to collect customer information in three areas on the site: The contact form, their free video session lottery, and booking. For the contact and lottery forms, I created two Google forms and embeded the custom action attributes accordingly. I then added my own Captcha equation. These together ensure customer information is stored securily, and prevents spam to my client.
AWS Hosting
Because we were relying on Google and Square to manage customers' personal and financial information, we were able to avoid having to manage our own server, and simple build it with static files. This kept the cost to almost nothing - a great alternative from the orignal Wordpress site.
